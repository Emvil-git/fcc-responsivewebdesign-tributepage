Pastor André Trocmé

(April 7, 1901 – June 5, 1971)

A Portrait of Pastor André Trocmé

About André Trocmé

Pastor André Trocmé was the spiritual leader of the Protestant congregation in the village of Le Chambon sur Lignon in the département of Haute-Loire in Southeastern France. His predecessor, Charles Guillon, had been elected mayor of the town in the early 1930s, and André Trocmé took over as spiritual leader of the congregation. When in June 1940 France was occupied and the Vichy regime was formed, Trocmé urged his congregants to shelter persecuted fugitives of “the people of the Bible.” In so doing, he followed in the footsteps of Guillon, who had educated the congregation in this spirit. This policy and the generosity of spirit of many congregants made Le Chambon and the surrounding villages a unique refuge in France, where many Jews, children and entire families, survived the war.

A timeline of events in André Trocmé's life

  • April 7, 1901 - André Trocmé was born in Saint-Quentin-en-Tourmont, a commune on the coast of northern France

  • 1926 - André Trocmé married Magda Grilli di Cortona

  • 1934 - André Trocmé and his family arrives at Le Chambon sur Lignon to accept the pastorate

  • 1938 - André Trocmé, along with the pacifist Reverend Edouard Theis L'Ecole Nouvelle Cévenol, a secondary school, in Le Chambon

  • 1940 - France falls to Nazi Germany, dividing the country into Occupied Zone (Paris) and the Unoccupied Zone, also known as Vichy France. Le Chambon is located in the latter

  • 1942 - Jewish Deportations begin under the Vichy Government; The residents of Le Chambon begin hiding and aiding Jewish refugees with the leadership of the Pastor

  • 1943 - André Trocmé, Edouard Theis, and public school head Roger Darcissac were arrested by Vichy authorities but were released after a month; Trocmé and Theis would go into hiding for 10 more months while his wife takes charge of helping refugees in Le Chambon

  • 1944 - The Allies take back France from Nazi Germany, liberating the country. By then, the community at Le Chambon had saved about 3,500 Jewish refugees from the Holocaust

  • 1950s - The Trocmés actively work as agents of pacifism throughout Europe

  • 1961 - André Trocmé publishes his book Jesus Christ and the nonviolent revolution

  • 1971 - Yad Vashem, the Holocaust memorial center in Israel recognizes André Trocmé as one of the Righteous among Nations. Madga Trocmé would receive the same honor in 1986

  • June 5, 1971 - André Trocmé dies in Geneva while serving as a pastor
